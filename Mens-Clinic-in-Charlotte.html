Hypogonadism can be treated with doctor-prescribed testosterone replacement therapy. This safe, and effective treatment has been a viable alternative for men experiencing some of the symptoms of low testosterone, as well as those associated with androgen and other hormone deficiencies. While testosterone replacement therapy remains a primary treatment option, there are some scenarios where hypogonadism can be reversed with other alternatives. In healthy men, there's a decrease in testosterone levels of about 1% per year after the age of 30. A few of the physiological areas that lead to disruption in natural testosterone production include the brain, testicles, and pituitary gland. Contrary to popular belief, testosterone levels are dynamic throughout the day, tending to peak in the morning and tail off at night. In addition to genetics, habits can lead to significantly lower or higher levels of testosterone production, including too little or too much exercise, certain illnesses, certain medications, and poor nutrition to name a few.
